Root causes of this issue

Root Causes #1

Disadvantaged students perform worse not because of bad teachers or school issues, but rather government reforms meant to improve disadvantaged students such as No child Left behind and Standardization Test has made it even worse for disadvantaged students.

  • Evidence 1: “NCLB has generated a range undesirable side effects -including large numbers of failing schools , narrowing of the curriculum, low morale among teachers who are facing pressure to achieve goals that they cannot meet, and, as has become abundantly clear in recent months by the cheating scandals in Atlanta and elsewhere, significant amounts of cheating by teachers under extreme pressure to raise student test scores.”"
  • Explanation 1: No Child Left Behind (also known as NCLB) has pressured teachers to inflate scores to meet the standard. It has closed schools that failed to meet the standard when it already has limited resources serving as low income schools.
  • Evidence 2: “The best U.S. evidence to date indicates that providing financial incentives for teachers to raise test scores does not lead to the desired results. In a recent experiment in which randomly assigned math teachers in grades 5-8 in Nashville were offered large bonuses for raising their students' test scores, for example, no differences emerged in the test scores of those teacher offered the incentive and those in the control group”
  • Explanation 2: Raising a teacher's salary based on scores doesn't improve a student's score. The incentives doesn't work as there is no different in both study groups

    • Root Causes #2

    Students having more choices of schools doesn't necessarily mean better results for student scores, with school vouchers and charter school has only caused more decline in student's scores.

  • Evidence 1: “The patterns were particularly dramatic in my 2007 study of charter schools in North Carolina with Robert Bifulco, where we found that the students in charter schools serving large proportions of minority students exhibited even greater adverse effects on student achievement than students in charter schools serving more middle class students”
  • Explanation 1: Charter schools have no or negative improvement for disadvantaged students. It also drains resources away from public schools that serve most of the disadvantaged students.
  • Evidence 2: “few charter schools appear to be doing a good job of promoting academic success among children from disadvantaged families, albeit often with the help of substantial additional resources from private philanthropists or from charismatic leaders.”
  • Explanation 2: Charter schools require more resources than public schools while only doing better when there is outside support other than the government coming in to support them. If those resources were given to a public school, then they could perform just as good as some of the charter schools

    • Implication for both root causes

  • Government reforms have already been tried to improve both students and disadvantaged students with little or negative improvement in student's education. The government needs a better way to approach this issue as the current solutions have only caused worse score feedback. The historical trend of the U.S. being ahead of other countries has drastically dropped and forces teachers to cheat to meet standards. More support for charter schools through school vouchers only makes disadvantaged students perform worse. School voucher itself has drains public resources away from public schools which still support the majority of disadvantaged students. The competition between charter and public only hurts students' education.
